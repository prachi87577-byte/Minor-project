🧪 Soil Science
Soil Health Monitoring

Healthy soil grows healthy crops. Soil health monitoring helps check pH level, moisture, and nutrients before planting. Farmers use sensors or lab kits to scan the land and prevent overuse of fertilisers.

💧 Water Tech
Smart Irrigation

Every drop of water counts. Smart irrigation helps water crops only when needed using moisture sensors and timers. It can reduce farm water use by 20–60% using drip and sprinkler systems.

Read More
🚁 AgriTech
Drone Spraying

Drone spraying uses UAVs to apply fertilisers and pesticides evenly across fields. Drones cover up to 50 acres per day, save 20–30% on spraying costs, and reduce worker chemical exposure.

Read More
🌿 Eco Farming
Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

IPM is an eco-friendly approach using natural, cultural, and chemical controls. It encourages natural predators and crop rotation, using chemicals only when necessary to reduce costs and environmental damage.

Read More
🏗️ Innovation
Controlled-Environment Agriculture (CEA)

CEA grows crops in fully controlled environments — greenhouses, indoor farms, or vertical setups — with precise control over light, temperature, humidity, and nutrients, enabling year-round farming.

Read More
📈 Business
Commercial Agriculture

Commercial agriculture produces crops and livestock for sale at scale. Farmers use mechanization, AI, and technology to profitably farm large tracts of land and connect with value chains and markets.

Read More
🐟 Aquaculture
Aquaculture and Hydroponics

Aquaculture is the farming of freshwater and marine products like shellfish, salmon, and seaweed. Multi-trophic aquaculture mimics natural ecosystems, farming complementary species together sustainably.

🌾 Farming Models
Intensive and Extensive Agriculture

Intensive farming maximises productivity using fertilisers and technology on small mechanised farms. Extensive farming uses more land with lower input costs, relying more on nature for sustainable yields.
